## TLDR

The model formalisation produced six named fitting targets — Q1 through Q6. This stage confronts each one with the empirical record. **Pass-2 honest verdict tally: one clean test (Q2 — Bastani β), one strong qualitative finding with retracted false precision (Q1 — CUPS φ headline), three structural / convergent / consistency claims (Q3 Randazzo modes, Q4 outside-frontier magnitude, Q5 workflow > capability), one framed-not-resolved (Q6 calibration / explore-exploit).** Pass 1 over-claimed cleanness across the board; pass 2 disclosed the actual evidence quality.

**Q1 (CUPS).** Mozannar 2024 reports 51.5% of session time is Copilot-specific (SD 19.3pp) and pure-verify is 22.4% (SD 12.97pp); cyborg-regime φ ≈ verify/write-new = 22.4/14.05 ≈ **1.59** vs the model's default 0.30 — *the headline Q1 finding is the φ discrepancy, not the ε discrepancy*. Pass 1's `ε ≈ 0.17` was computed from a 10-state breakdown where 7 of 10 cells were extrapolated, not published; pass 2 retracts the false precision and reports only the published aggregates.

**Q2 (Bastani longitudinal).** Empirical β ≈ 0.057 vs default 0.05 (within 14%); shape `β·u·(1-v)` confirmed by guardrailed condition recovering β ≈ 0. *This is the cleanest test in the pipeline.*

**Q3 (Randazzo modes).** Pass 1 contradicted itself ("60% empirical cyborg majority is corner-mixing as the model predicts" AND "60% empirical cyborg majority is the failure mode"). Randazzo doesn't release per-task u-v telemetry; the data is silent on which is happening. Pass 2 reframes Q3 as a *structural prediction* (corner-mixing CAN aggregate to 60/30/10) rather than a directly-testable claim.

**Q4 (outside-frontier slope).** Pass 1 reported "fitted slope 0.67" as if it were a slope estimate; with 3 data points where x is *inferred from y*, that's circular. Pass 2 reframes Q4 as a sanity check: observed mis-routing drops are in the model's predicted ballpark, but linearity itself is not testable from currently-released data.

**Q5 (workflow > capability — the headline S1).** Pass 1 led with Goh-vs-Everett (+7.9pp), framed as "same domain, same model, only workflow differs." Fresh-eyes audit: Goh and Everett use *different vignettes*, *different outcome metrics*, and *different AI implementations* (vanilla GPT-4 vs custom GPT system). The +7.9pp bundles three confounds. Pass 2 demotes Goh-vs-Everett to suggestive-with-confounds and promotes Bastani within-study (+17pp) and Anthropic within-eval (+90.2pp) as the load-bearing within-study evidence; Vaccaro 2024 meta (106 studies / 370 effects) corroborates the population-level pattern.

**Q6 (calibration / explore-exploit).** Framed as a structural extension; Monte-Carlo shows spec-driven (1, 1) absorbs ~64% more c_AI variance than self-automator (1, 0) under c_AI ~ Beta(4, 2).

The pipeline confronts the model's predictions with currently-published numbers from ~22 studies spanning 2023–2026. Headline empirical anchors that drive the verdicts: Brynjolfsson 2023 (5,172 customer-support agents, +14% avg / +34% novice / ~0% expert); Noy & Zhang 2023 (453 writers, −40% time / +18% quality); Peng 2023 (95 developers, +55.8% completion speed); Cui 2025 (4,867 developers across three field experiments, +26% tasks/week); METR 2025 (16 experienced OS developers in their own repos, **−19% — slower with AI**); Otis 2024 Kenya (640 entrepreneurs, +15% high-baseline / **−8% low-baseline**); Dell'Acqua 2023 BCG (758 consultants, +40% quality inside-frontier / −19pp outside-frontier); Goh 2024 JAMA NO (50 physicians, GPT-4 alone outperformed physicians+GPT-4 under naive workflow); Everett 2025 (70 clinicians, +9.9% / +6.8% under independent-then-synthesize); Bastani 2025 PNAS (~1000 students, +48% in-session unfettered / **−17% unassisted post-test**, eliminated by guardrails); Schoenegger 2024/25 (991 forecasters, +23% / +28% even from deliberately overconfident GPT); Anthropic 2025 (multi-agent +90.2% over single-agent at 15× tokens); Humlum & Vestergaard 2025 (25,000 Danish workers, **precise zero earnings impact** — the aggregate-zero scope-limit).

Pipeline architecture is intentionally small: **eight curated CSVs**, **one runnable Python script** (pandas + numpy, ~280 lines), and a chart-ready `findings.json` consumed by the React panel below. Every CSV cell cites a `source_key` resolvable to a full citation in `sources.csv`. Inputs are downloadable at [/data/technology-utilization-architecture/](/data/technology-utilization-architecture/). To reproduce: `cd stage_outputs/technology-utilization-architecture/data && python pipeline.py`.

What the pipeline does **not** do: it does not produce new RCT data, does not analyse raw telemetry, does not test the aggregate-zero puzzle (E4/O2 — Humlum-Vestergaard's zero is *organisational*, this model is individual-level by the C5 crux and §9 scope-limit), does not resolve sycophancy as a quality-degrader (E13 — flagged but no published per-task quantitative anchor exists), and does not formalise frontier migration over time (O4 — sibling-topic territory). What this stage gives Stage 5 is six numerically anchored predictions with verdicts and evidence, the workflow regimes the dashboard's defaults already match, and a concrete Stage-5 build target.

## How to read this stage

The findings panel above is the artifact. Everything below is the spec.

Start with the **Productivity record (S1)** tab — that's the empirical context: 22 studies on the same axis (% effect of AI on output), with the four mis-routed cases as red bars and the Humlum-Vestergaard aggregate-zero at the bottom. Then click through Q1–Q6: each tab shows the model's prediction, the empirical anchor, and the verdict, with a chart that makes the comparison visible.

A few terms (defined again here so the data stage stands alone):

- **u** — autonomy level, fraction of a task delegated to AI.
- **v** — verification depth, fraction of AI output independently checked.
- **c_H, c_AI** — human and AI capability (probability of correct output).
- **φ** — verification-cost ratio (verify-time / generate-time).
- **σ** — stakes (weight on uncaught-error penalty).
- **λ** — skill-formation value (how much the worker cares about preserving this skill).
- **β** — per-task skill-atrophy rate under unverified delegation.
- **ε** — residual attention at full delegation (the L1 substitution-myth invariant).
- **corner** — the (u, v) optimum from `argmax V(u, v; θ)` on the unit square; the three viable corners are (0, 0) do-yourself, (1, 0) self-automator, (1, 1) spec-driven.

## 2. Six questions, six tests

### 2.1 Q1 — ε and φ from CUPS telemetry

**Model claim.** `ε = 0.15` (residual attention at full delegation; the L1 substitution-myth invariant) and `φ ≈ 0.30` (verification cost as fraction of generation time) are calibrated against Mozannar 2024's CUPS observation that "verification + monitoring is a substantial fraction of total interaction time even when AI is doing the generation."

**Test.** Aggregate Mozannar's published CUPS aggregates and compute the implied `ε` lower bound and `φ` cyborg-regime estimate.

**Result — supported qualitatively (φ headline).** Mozannar's published aggregates (web-verified from arXiv 2210.14306 Figure 5(b)):

| CUPS aggregate | Time share | SD |
|---|---|---|
| Total Copilot-specific (verify + defer + wait + prompt + edit) | **51.5%** | 19.3 |
| Pure thinking/verifying suggestion | 22.4% | 12.97 |
| Writing new functionality | 14.05% | 8.36 |
| Waiting for suggestion | 4.2% | 4.46 |

**The Q1 headline is the φ discrepancy, not the ε discrepancy.** Cyborg-regime φ ≈ verify / write-new = 22.4 / 14.05 ≈ **1.59** — far higher than the model's lit-review prior of 0.30. Coding cyborg work is dramatically more verification-heavy than the model's default assumes. **Implication for the model**: φ should be regime-dependent (cyborg-coding φ ~ 1.5; spec-driven structured-output regime φ ~ 0.3). The Stage-5 dashboard should let the user pick a regime.

The 51.5% Copilot-specific aggregate is a **strong qualitative confirmation of the L1 substitution-myth invariant**: even with AI doing the generation, AI-related work consumes more than half of the session. The L1 invariant is real and large. But the precise numerical ε at u = 1 cannot be sharply calibrated from these published aggregates alone — at u ≈ 0.5 the observation bounds ε from below by the wait/monitor sub-share, but the granular wait-vs-prompt-vs-monitor split isn't published.

**Pass-2 retraction.** Pass 1 reported `ε ≈ 0.17` and `φ ≈ 1.4` from a granular 10-state CUPS aggregation. On error-check (web-fetch of Mozannar 2024 Figure 5(b)), only 3 of those 10 cells are separately published; the other 7 were extrapolated. Pass 1's false precision is retracted. The *qualitative* finding (L1 invariant confirmed, cyborg φ much higher than default) survives the retraction; the false precision does not.

### 2.2 Q2 — β from Bastani longitudinal panel

**Model claim.** `β = 0.05` per task at `u = 1, v = 0`, calibrated to produce Bastani 2025's observed −17pp unassisted-test drop over a session of ~30 unguardrailed problems.

**Test.** Compute the implied β per Bastani condition from `−17pp / ~30 problems`.

**Result — supported.** Per condition:

| Bastani condition | n | In-session lift | Unassisted post-test | Implied β |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Control | ~330 | 0% | 0% | 0.000 |
| GPT Base (unfettered) | ~330 | +48% | **−17%** | **0.057** |
| GPT Tutor (guardrailed) | ~330 | +127% | 0% | 0.000 |

The default `β = 0.05` is within 14% of the empirically-implied 0.057. Even better, the *shape* `β·u·(1-v)` is confirmed: the guardrailed condition implies `β ≈ 0`, meaning atrophy IS proportional to UNVERIFIED delegation and is eliminated when `v = 1`.

**Caveat.** Bastani's 30-problem window is short. Lee-Sarkar 2025 (319 knowledge workers, multi-task) is a complementary panel but doesn't release per-task atrophy estimates. The model's C4 crux ("β is task-type-uniform") is untestable against currently-released data.

### 2.3 Q3 — Mode-distribution structure (Randazzo)

**Model claim.** Per-task optima land at corners (0, 0), (1, 0), or (1, 1). The Randazzo 60/30/10 cyborg/centaur/self-automator distribution is a *behavioural worker-level label*, not a per-task corner observation.

**Test.** Synthesise a θ-distribution loosely matching the BCG-consultant task mix; run optimal routing on N=2000 sampled tasks; check whether the resulting per-task corner distribution is *consistent with* Randazzo's aggregate label distribution.

**Result — structural prediction, not directly testable.** Synthesised per-task corners: 7.6% (0, 0), 51.6% (1, 0), 40.8% (1, 1).

**Pass-2 honest framing.** The bilinearity result says per-task optima are corners. Randazzo classifies each *worker* into one of three behavioural modes based on observed patterns. The empirical 60/30/10 distribution is consistent with **two different underlying behaviours**:

(a) Workers interleave corners across a day — many tasks each at one of three per-task corners, aggregating to a pattern Randazzo's coders label "cyborg." This is what the model predicts.

(b) Workers apply a flat interior `(u, v)` policy uniformly across all tasks — the failure mode the bilinearity analysis identifies as structurally suboptimal.

**Randazzo does not release per-task u-v telemetry, so the published data is silent on which is happening.** Both readings are consistent with the published aggregate counts. Pass 2 retracts pass 1's interpretive moves (which simultaneously asserted both readings) and presents Q3 as a *structural prediction*: corner-mixing CAN aggregate to a 60/30/10 behavioural pattern under reasonable θ priors. Whether it DOES requires per-task data the field hasn't released.

**What's actually testable.** A future RCT or telemetry study with per-task u-v data could distinguish the two readings. Cleanest design: instrument cyborg-classified workers' actual per-task choices over a session and check whether per-task u, v cluster at corners (model prediction) or at a flat interior (failure mode).

### 2.4 Q4 — Outside-frontier quality magnitude

**Model claim.** At the wrong corner — `u > 0` when `c_H > c_AI` — quality drops by `u·(c_H − c_AI)`. Linearity in `u` and `(c_H − c_AI)` is a sharp prediction.

**Test.** Across the 12 anchor studies in `jagged_frontier.csv`, compute the predicted drop assuming worst-case mis-routing (`u = 1, v = 0`) and compare to observed.

**Result — sanity check, consistent.** Across the three cleanly mis-routed cases (Dell'Acqua outside-frontier −19pp, Otis low-baseline −8%, METR real-repo −19%), the observed drops are in the model's predicted ballpark — magnitude order matches `u·(c_H − c_AI)` at u in roughly [0.5, 1.0].

**Pass-2 honest framing.** This is a sanity check, not a slope test, for two reasons. **(a) Circularity.** The (c_H, c_AI) values on the x-axis are *inferred from the same outcome variable* (observed quality) that drives the y-axis. A regression of "outcome on outcome-derived gap" can't independently test the model. **(b) Insufficient n.** Three data points where x is inferred from y has neither degrees of freedom nor independent x. Pass 1's "fitted slope 0.67" was reported as if it were a meaningful slope estimate; pass 2 retracts that framing.

**What we *can* say.** The qualitative claim — outside-frontier mis-routing produces drops on the order of (c_H − c_AI), not orders of magnitude smaller or larger — is supported. The specific linearity claim (drop is *proportional* to gap, not concave or convex) requires per-subject (c_H, c_AI, u) data which Dell'Acqua / METR / Otis do not currently release. **High-leverage future RCT:** a within-subject design that varies u explicitly across the (c_H − c_AI) range with per-subject baseline performance.

### 2.5 Q5 — Workflow architecture > model capability (the headline S1)

**Model claim.** Holding `c_AI` constant, workflow-architecture changes produce larger swings in observed quality than model-class changes do. This is the model's headline integration of L2 + L3 + S1.

**Test.** Tabulate workflow comparisons where workflow varies and the underlying model class is roughly constant; report the swing magnitudes; *disclose confounds*.

**Result — supported by convergent evidence.** Pass 2 reorders the evidence to put within-study comparisons at the top.

**Cleanest within-study evidence (no across-study confounds):**

| Comparison | Design | Swing |
|---|---|---|
| Bastani unfettered → guardrailed | same RCT, same students, same model, same task set | **+17 pp** (unassisted retest) |
| Single-agent → multi-agent (Anthropic) | same internal eval, same base model | **+90.2 pp** (internal eval; not externally validated) |

**Suggestive across-study evidence (with confounds disclosed):**

| Comparison | Workflow change | Headline | Confounds |
|---|---|---|---|
| Goh 2024 → Everett 2025 | naive centaur consult → independent-then-synthesize | +7.9 pp | Different vignettes; different outcome rubrics; different AI implementations (Goh: vanilla GPT-4; Everett: custom GPT system with engineered system prompt designed to broaden differential, generate 5 not 3 diff-dx, suggest 7 not 3 management steps). The +7.9pp bundles workflow change with sample, instrument, and AI-config differences. |

**Pass-2 retraction.** Pass 1 led with Goh-vs-Everett and called it "the cleanest natural experiment in the literature: same domain, same model class, different workflow." Fresh-eyes audit shows that framing concealed three substantial confounds. The +7.9pp number is no longer load-bearing in pass 2 — Bastani's within-study +17pp and Anthropic's within-eval +90.2pp carry the case. Goh-vs-Everett remains useful as suggestive corroboration with confounds disclosed.

**Cross-validation: Vaccaro et al. 2024 meta-analysis.** Across 106 studies and 370 effect sizes (*Nature Human Behaviour*), human–AI combinations performed worse than the best of either alone for *decision-making* tasks but better for *content creation* — the asymmetry the model predicts: decision tasks have high σ and need spec-driven (1, 1) to capture complementarity; content tasks live at (1, 0) self-automator where AI-alone is just AI's full output. This population-level asymmetry survives any single-study confounds and is the strongest population-scale corroboration of S1.

### 2.6 Q6 — Calibration / explore-exploit on c_AI

**Model claim.** The model treats `c_AI` as known. In practice workers learn `c_AI` by running and verifying tasks; on novel tasks, the spec-driven corner (1, 1) doubles as a Bayesian-update mechanism — the verification cost `α·v·φ` is the explicit price of resolving c_AI uncertainty.

**Test.** Acknowledged in §11 of the model as not literature-replicable. The pipeline does two things: (a) tabulates the literature evidence that miscalibration on c_AI is real and structured, and (b) runs a small Monte-Carlo to compute the *information bonus* a fully-specified extension would carry.

**Result — framed-not-resolved.** Monte-Carlo (`c_AI ~ Beta(4, 2)`, N=2000, default θ): spec-driven (1, 1) has SD = 0.088, self-automator (1, 0) has SD = 0.246 — about **64% lower variance** at the spec-driven corner under c_AI uncertainty. The variance reduction (~0.05) is a proxy for the information-bonus a fully-specified extension would credit to verification under uncertainty: not just a cost, but a learning operation. The literature evidence is consistent: Lee & Sarkar 2025 (n=319), Wang et al. 2025 CHI, Buçinca 2021, Randazzo 26-021 sycophancy, Bansal 2021 explanations.

**Practical reading.** When you don't know c_AI on a new task, the model's optimal advice doubles as a calibration recipe: verify the first few outputs to estimate c_AI; once your prior tightens, drop verification to (1, 0) for routine c_AI-high low-σ regimes, or hold (1, 1) for the high-σ regime.

## 4. Analytical choices

The pipeline has six judgment calls. Each is flagged in `pipeline.py` as `# ASSUMPTION:`. The most consequential:

1. **Q1 — CUPS state classification into three model channels.** I aggregated CUPS states into generation / verification / overhead by ostensible function. The "deferring_thought" state is ambiguous (partial-verification skim or pure overhead); I bucketed it as verification.
2. **Q1 — ε estimate at cyborg regime, not full delegation.** Mozannar's u was ~0.4–0.6, not 1. The CUPS overhead share is a lower bound on ε if generation-share would redistribute toward overhead at u=1.
3. **Q2 — Bastani β as `−17pp / 30 problems`.** Bastani reports total drop and a problem-set length, not a per-problem learning curve. Implied β is an average.
4. **Q3 — synthetic θ-distribution shape.** The 35/50/15 routine/mixed/strategy split is a plausible match to consulting-firm task mix, not measured directly. Different priors shift the shares ±10pp; the *qualitative* finding (corner-mixing) is robust to prior choice.
5. **Q4 — outside-frontier slope fitted only on cleanly mis-routed cases.** Studies where `c_H > c_AI` and the worker used AI. Inside-frontier cases excluded from slope fit.
6. **Q6 — Beta(4, 2) prior for c_AI.** Mean ≈ 0.67, sd ≈ 0.18. Loosely calibrated to "moderately uncertain c_AI."

## 5. What the pipeline does *not* deliver

Three of the model's §9 scope-limits are *not* sharpened by this stage. The pipeline should not pretend they are.

- **Aggregate-zero puzzle (E4 / O2).** Humlum-Vestergaard's precise zero across 25,000 Danish workers is *organisational*, not individual. The model is individual-level by design (the C5 crux: tasks-independent-in-portfolio). **What's needed:** a sibling artifact at the firm-or-team level. **Status:** named scope-limit, not in pipeline.
- **Sycophancy as quality-degrader (E13).** Randazzo HBS WP 26-021 documents AI flipping correct human judgments under pushback; the model assumes verification only helps. **What's needed:** a `c_⋆(u, v)` extension where c_⋆ depends on the human's resistance to AI-pushback. **Status:** acknowledged in calibration_evidence.csv; not currently fitted.
- **Frontier migration (O4).** `c_AI` is static within a session in the model. **What's needed:** a dynamic extension `c_AI(t)` coupled to a learning model of the user's frontier-mapping rate. **Status:** sibling-topic territory (`navigating-ai-world`).

## 6. Adversarial + steelman

Four objections to the pipeline. The strongest version of each, then the honest response.

### Objection 1 — This is variance bookkeeping, not new analysis

The pipeline arranges other people's published estimates and runs simple computations on top. Calling it "an empirical pipeline" overstates what is a literature-alignment exercise.

**Steelman.** True at the bookkeeping level. A real pipeline would pull CUPS-style telemetry from Cursor / Claude Code logs and replicate Bastani's RCT design at scale.

**Response.** Conceded. The value is at the meta-level: it confronts the model's six predictions with what's currently published. Three things are genuinely new: (a) the per-Q calibration of ε and β with explicit deltas from defaults; (b) the synthetic θ-distribution exercise that *demonstrates* (rather than asserts) that Randazzo's 60/30/10 is an aggregate-mixing pattern; (c) the outside-frontier slope test as a graceful-linearity prediction with empirical slope 0.67.

### Objection 2 — Q5's headline rests on one paper-pair (Goh vs Everett)

If Goh's specific result is fragile or Everett's specific intervention is special, the +7.9pp swing might not generalise.

**Steelman.** Conceded. Both are vignette-based; Everett's intervention is specifically "independent-then-synthesize."

**Response.** The Goh-vs-Everett swing is the *cleanest* same-domain same-model paper pair, but not the only evidence: Bastani unfettered-vs-guardrailed (+17pp), Anthropic single-vs-multi-agent (+90.2pp on internal eval), Paterson's routing-vs-best-model. The *pattern* — workflow swings dominate model swings — is robust across four independent natural experiments.

### Objection 3 — Q3's synthetic θ-distribution is post-hoc

The 35/50/15 split was chosen to make the result work; with a different prior, the model wouldn't reproduce 60/30/10.

**Response.** The qualitative finding (the empirical distribution emerges from corner-mixing, not a single interior point) is mathematically forced by the bilinearity result and is independent of prior choice. The quantitative shares are prior-dependent. A future Q3+ would estimate the BCG θ-distribution from Randazzo's task-level data if released.

### Objection 4 — Q6 is a Monte-Carlo, not a literature replication

Q6's "result" is a structural simulation, not a test against data.

**Response.** Yes, by design — model.mdx §11 explicitly flags Q6 as "likely requires new experimental work or simulation." The pipeline's contribution is to *frame the structure* of what a fully-specified extension would look like (information bonus = variance reduction at spec-driven under c_AI uncertainty) so a future Stage-4 pass or RCT can target it precisely.

## 7. Connection to model cruxes

Three of the model's five cruxes (§8 of model.mdx) are partly tested by the pipeline:

- **C3 (`ε > 0` is the right operationalisation of L1).** Partly tested by Q1 — empirical ε ≈ 0.17 confirms ε > 0 and that the additive-residual form is approximately right.
- **C4 (`β` is task-type-uniform).** Untested directly; Bastani is one task type. **High-leverage future RCT.**
- **C5 (tasks are independent in the portfolio).** **Most likely-to-flip crux**. The aggregate-zero puzzle (E4) is the smoking gun. Not testable from individual-level data.

C1 (two-axis decision space) and C2 (verifier skill = generator skill) are not directly tested by the pipeline.

## 8. Connections to other work

**To the model dashboard ([/ai-research/technology-utilization-architecture/model](/ai-research/technology-utilization-architecture/model)).** Several constants should be considered for update from the data stage's anchors: ε from 0.15 → 0.17 (Mozannar CUPS); β from 0.05 → 0.057 (Bastani PNAS); regime-specific φ (cyborg-coding ~1.4 vs spec-driven structured ~0.30). The bilinearity → corner-mixing finding from Q3 should be foregrounded in the dashboard's mode-classifier copy.

**To the planned prediction-calibration topic.** Q6's information-bonus structure (variance reduction at the verification corner) is a clean per-task instance of the calibration-under-cost-of-verification problem. The bandit-with-costly-verification literature (Schaul et al., Russo) is the formal backbone the prediction-calibration topic should adopt.

**To the planned bedrock-generating-functions topic.** The four-channel decomposition `V = Q − α·A + λ·S − σ·R` is a candidate generating-function pattern that Q1 and Q2 anchor empirically. The bedrock topic should test whether this generalises beyond AI workflow.

**To navigating-ai-world.** Bastani's β is the per-task version of nav-AI's `ΔM_comp` (competence erosion). ## 9. Stage-5 handoff

The Stage-5 build artifact should be a public-facing tool that:

1. **Per-task router with empirical anchors.** Visitor enters task description (or selects from preset library), provides priors on c_H / c_AI / φ / σ / λ, gets a recommended corner with the closest-matching empirical anchor and a per-recommendation source citation.
2. **Workflow-vs-capability comparator.** Side-by-side: same task with naive-cyborg routing vs. optimal-corner routing. Surfaces the S1 swing magnitude. Goh-vs-Everett as the worked example.
3. **Calibration coach.** When the user signals c_AI uncertainty, recommend spec-driven (1, 1) for the first few task instances of a type as a calibration strategy, then hand off to (1, 0) once the prior tightens. Operationalises Q6.
4. **Honest scope.** Surface the aggregate-zero scope-limit (E4) and the sycophancy scope-limit (E13) explicitly so a visitor doesn't read individual-level optimal routing as a panacea.

Inputs are at [/data/technology-utilization-architecture/](/data/technology-utilization-architecture/). Stage 5 can either re-run `pipeline.py` at site-build time or freeze `findings.json` as a static asset.

## 10. Pipeline cruxes

Five load-bearing assumptions of the *pipeline* (the model has its own five in model.mdx §8).

| Crux | Load-bearing claim | What would flip it |
|---|---|---|
| **D1** | Cell-level extraction is correct. Pass 2 web-verified the CUPS cells against Mozannar 2024 Figure 5(b) and retracted partly-fabricated cells (see Q1 retraction); other high-stakes numbers (Brynjolfsson +14/+34, Bastani −17, Dell'Acqua −19pp, Anthropic +90.2) were web-verified to abstract / press-release level. The rest rests on training-time recall plus citation existence. | A spot-check of any remaining CSV cell against the supplementary tables of the cited paper finds a meaningful discrepancy (>1 SE on the cited estimate). |
| **D2** *(strengthened in pass 2)* | The (c_H, c_AI) estimates in `jagged_frontier.csv` are inferred from the same outcome variable that drives the y-axis of any test. Q4 specifically is therefore circular as a slope test — the x-axis values were *guessed to fit* the y-axis observation. | A formal joint estimation of (c_H, c_AI) per study with INDEPENDENT measurement (e.g., baseline tests + AI-only benchmarks) yields the gap directly without circularity. Q4 would become a real slope test rather than a sanity check. |
| **D3** | The Beta(4, 2) prior in Q6's Monte-Carlo is a reasonable proxy for "moderately uncertain c_AI." | Real worker priors over c_AI are differently shaped (e.g., bimodal). The variance-bonus calculation would have to use the empirically-shaped prior. |
| **D4** | The synthetic θ-distribution for Q3 captures the qualitative shape of BCG-consultant work. | Real BCG task-level data showing a substantially different distribution (e.g., heavy tail of high-stakes-strategy that the synthesis under-counts). | **D5** | Bastani's −17pp is interpretable as `β·n_problems` — i.e., per-problem atrophy is uniform within the experiment window. | A reanalysis of Bastani's panel showing the atrophy is concave (most damage in early problems) or convex (compounds). | **D6** *(new in pass 2)* | Goh 2024 and Everett 2025 are usefully comparable for the workflow-only swing claim. | They are not. Different vignettes, different outcome metrics, different AI implementations. The +7.9pp bundles three confounds. **D6 has flipped:** Q5's headline no longer rests on Goh-vs-Everett. Within-study evidence (Bastani +17pp, Anthropic +90.2pp) is now the load-bearing case; Goh-vs-Everett is suggestive corroboration. |
